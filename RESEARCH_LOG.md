# Research Log

This document records the methodological decisions made throughout the research process. It is intended both as a personal reference and as a transparency resource for researchers interested in replicating or building upon this work.

---

## Data Collection

### FUNAI Negative Certificates

**How data was obtained:**
The 78 certificates were obtained by submitting a direct email request to FUNAI's archive department. FUNAI staff digitized the original process files — including both the land requests and the certificates themselves — and delivered them electronically. The digitization and delivery process took approximately 2 to 3 months.

**Nature of the corpus:**
The 78 certificates are not a statistically representative sample of the estimated 1,400 certificates issued during this period. They constitute a documentary corpus — the documents FUNAI selected and made available in response to the request. No selection criterion was applied by the researcher; the selection was made by FUNAI. The selective nature of institutional archive access is itself analytically relevant: what was not provided is as significant as what was.

**Data entry:**
All 34 columns were filled in manually by the researcher, reading each document individually. No automated extraction or OCR was used at this stage. This process ensured careful reading of each certificate but introduced the possibility of human error in transcription.

**Usage restrictions:**
The dataset contains personal information (names of individuals, company names, property details). A usage agreement signed with FUNAI restricts the data to academic research purposes and prohibits public redistribution. Raw data is therefore not available in this repository. A data dictionary and a synthetic sample dataset are provided instead.

---

### Press Reports

**How data was obtained:**
The 105 press reports represent an exhaustive survey of the indigenous press archive held by the Centro de Referência Virtual Indígena, available through the Armazém Memória platform (https://armazemmemoria.com.br/). All relevant reports found in this archive that referenced negative certificates or related land conflicts were included. No reports were excluded based on content or perspective.

**Nature of the corpus:**
The corpus covers the period from 1972 to 2009, extending well beyond the period of certificate issuance (1970–1990). This temporal span allows analysis of how conflicts generated by the certificates unfolded over subsequent decades.

## Coordinate Processing

### Datum identification and assumption

The certificates were produced between 1970 and 1990, a period during which the standard geodetic datum in Brazil was SAD69 (South American Datum 1969). Most certificates did not explicitly specify a datum. Given the historical period of production, SAD69 was assumed for all certificates where the datum was not stated in the original document. This decision was recorded in the `Datum Assumido` column of the dataset.

### Coordinate conversion

All coordinates were converted from SAD69 to WGS84 to enable compatibility with contemporary GIS software and web mapping tools. Conversion was performed using Python scripts available in the `scripts/` folder of this repository. The converted vertices are stored in the `Vértices Convertidos` column.

### Coordinate formats

The original documents used a variety of coordinate formats and systems (UTM, geographic degrees, etc.). The format and system found in each document were recorded in the `Coordenadas Formato` and `Coordenadas Sistema` columns respectively. Researcher notes on edge cases and conversion decisions are recorded in the `Observação Coordenadas` column.

## Known Limitations

- The 78 certificates represent approximately 5.6% of the estimated total of 1,400 issued. Findings should be interpreted as pertaining to this corpus, not generalized to the full universe of certificates.
- Manual data entry introduces the possibility of transcription errors.
- The press report corpus is limited to sources available in the Armazém Memória archive and may not capture all relevant coverage, particularly from regional or local publications not indexed in that platform.
- 30 of the 78 certificates make no mention of any indigenous people or territory. It is unclear whether this reflects the content of the original documents or gaps in the available archive.

## Exploratory Analysis — Signatories, Institutional Profile, and Geographic Distribution

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Dataset:** `certidoes_negativas_funai_v7.csv`
**Notebook:** `exploratory_analysis2.ipynb`

### Signatories
Oscar Jerônimo Bandeira de Mello is the leading signatory in the corpus, concentrating the highest number of negative certificates issued. Particularly relevant given his role as FUNAI president at two different moments during the civil-military dictatorship.

### Institutional Profile
71.8% of certificates were signed by military personnel; 28.2% by civilians. This provides empirical confirmation of FUNAI's militarization during the period and of the negative certificates as an instrument of the military apparatus.

### Geographic Distribution
Mato Grosso (MT) and Pará (PA) concentrate the largest share of certificates, consistent with the expansion of the agricultural frontier and large infrastructure projects in the Amazon region during the period. No anomalous municipal concentration detected; distribution is consistent with the state-level pattern.

## Exploratory Analysis — Temporal Distribution, Press Coverage, and Cross-Dataset Visualization

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Datasets:** `certidoes_negativas_funai_v7.csv`, `reportagens_certidoes_negativas_v4.csv`
**Notebook:** `exploratory_analysis2.ipynb`

### Annual Frequency of Negative Certificates (1970–1990)
Bar chart showing the yearly distribution of certificate issuance. Reveals periods of acceleration and relative silence within the corpus.

### Certificates by Year and Signatory
Heatmap (year × signatory) showing which officials were most active in each period. Prior to plotting, inconsistencies in the `Nome do Signatário` column were corrected and the dataset was saved as `certidoes_negativas_funai_v7.csv`.

### Certificates by Year and State
Bar chart (stacked by state) showing the geographic distribution of certificates over time. Provides visual evidence for the hypothesis that certificate issuance followed the expansion of the agricultural frontier: concentrated in Mato Grosso (MT) during the 1970s and shifting toward Pará (PA) in the 1980s. A focused MT × PA comparison was produced to make this pattern explicit.

### Annual Frequency of Press Coverage (1972–2009)
Bar chart showing the yearly distribution of press reports. Prior to plotting, inconsistencies in the `Nome do Jornal ou Revista` column were corrected across multiple iterations and the dataset was saved as `reportagens_certidoes_negativas_v4.csv`.

### Press Coverage by Outlet — Overall Ranking
Horizontal bar chart ranking all outlets by total number of reports in the corpus.

### Press Coverage by Year and Outlet
Heatmap (year × outlet) showing which vehicles dominated coverage in each period.

### Certificates × Press Coverage — Dual-Axis Temporal Chart
Side-by-side bar chart overlaying certificate issuance (1970–1990) and press coverage (1972–2009) on a shared time axis, with independent Y-axes. Visually captures the relationship between institutional action and public visibility over time.
